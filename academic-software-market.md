<p><p>The academic software market can be divided into two primary types: cloud-based and on-premises. Cloud-based software is hosted on remote servers, allowing users to access applications via the internet, offering flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness, with easier updates and collaboration. In contrast, on-premises software is installed locally on individual computers or servers, providing greater control over data and security but requiring higher upfront costs and maintenance responsibilities. Both types cater to the diverse needs of educational institutions.</p></p>
