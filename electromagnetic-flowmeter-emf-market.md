<p><p>Electromagnetic Flowmeters (EMF) are precision instruments utilized for measuring the flow rate of conductive liquids in various industrial applications. These devices operate based on Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction, where a magnetic field generates a voltage proportional to the flow rate of the liquid. EMFs are widely recognized for their accuracy, reliability, and ability to measure a broad range of fluid types, making them essential in sectors such as water treatment, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, and food and beverage processing.</p><p>The EMF market is expected to witness substantial growth, projected to grow at a CAGR of 12.5% during the forecast period.
