<p><p>The OBD2 code reader market comprises two primary types: wired and wireless. Wired OBD2 readers connect directly to a vehicle’s diagnostic port, offering reliable data transmission and often requiring no external power source. In contrast, wireless OBD2 readers use Bluetooth or Wi-Fi technology to sync with smartphones or tablets, providing greater convenience and mobility. While wired options are typically favored for their stability and ease of use, wireless devices appeal to users seeking versatility and remote access to diagnostic information.</p></p>
