<p><p>The dielectric materials market comprises various types, including calcium titanate, magnesium titanate, and barium titanate, each serving unique applications. Calcium titanate is known for its high dielectric constant and ferroelectric properties, making it suitable for capacitors and sensors. Magnesium titanate offers excellent thermal stability and low losses, ideal for high-frequency applications. Barium titanate exhibits piezoelectric characteristics, useful in transducers and actuators. Other materials include polymers and ceramics, catering to diverse industries such as electronics, telecommunications, and automotive.</p></p>
