<p><p>The photovoltaic half-cell module market encompasses two primary types: monocrystalline half-cell modules and polycrystalline half-cell modules. Monocrystalline half-cells, made from single-crystal silicon, offer higher efficiency and better performance in low-light conditions, making them ideal for space-constrained installations. In contrast, polycrystalline half-cell modules, composed of multiple silicon crystals, are typically more cost-effective but slightly less efficient. Both types leverage the half-cell design, which enhances energy output and reduces shading losses, driving growth in various solar energy applications.</p></p>
