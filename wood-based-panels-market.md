<p><ul><li>Particleboard (PB)</li><li>Medium Density Fibreboard (MDF)</li><li>High Density Fibreboard (HDF)</li><li>Oriented Strand Board (OSB)</li><li>Plywood</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wood-based panels market includes several types, each with distinct characteristics and uses. Particleboard (PB) is made from wood particles and is cost-effective for furniture. Medium Density Fibreboard (MDF) offers a smooth surface for applications like cabinetry due to its denser composition. High Density Fibreboard (HDF) is denser than MDF, making it suitable for flooring. Oriented Strand Board (OSB) is engineered from strands of wood, providing structural strength in construction. Plywood, composed of thin layers of wood veneer, is versatile for furniture and building projects.</p></p>
