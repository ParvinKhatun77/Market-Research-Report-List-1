<p><ul><li>Multiply Distilled</li><li>Singly Distilled</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Japanese shochu market is primarily categorized into two types based on the distillation process: multiply distilled and singly distilled. Multiply distilled shochu, often known as "korui," undergoes multiple distillations, resulting in a lighter flavor and higher purity. In contrast, singly distilled shochu, or "honkaku," retains more character from its base ingredients, offering a richer taste profile. This distinction reflects consumer preferences, with honkaku shochu appealing to those seeking traditional, nuanced flavors, while korui targets a broader audience seeking versatility.</p></p>
