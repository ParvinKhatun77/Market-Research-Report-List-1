<p><p>The cardiac dysrhythmia medications market is divided into two main types: oral antiarrhythmic and intravenous antiarrhythmic medications. Oral antiarrhythmics, taken in pill form, are commonly used for chronic management of arrhythmias, providing long-term control and convenience. Intravenous antiarrhythmics, administered through injection, are typically used in acute settings to quickly stabilize patients experiencing severe arrhythmias. Both types are essential for effective treatment, but they cater to different clinical situations and patient needs.</p></p>
