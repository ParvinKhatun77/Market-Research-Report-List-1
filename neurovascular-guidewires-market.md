<p><p>The neurovascular guidewires market comprises two primary types: solid guide wires and wrapped guide wires. Solid guide wires offer high strength and stability, making them suitable for navigating complex vascular paths. They are often used in procedures requiring precise positioning. Wrapped guide wires, on the other hand, feature a coating that enhances flexibility and reduces friction, improving maneuverability in tortuous vessels. Both types play critical roles in neurovascular interventions, facilitating safe access and treatment of cerebral and spinal conditions.</p></p>
