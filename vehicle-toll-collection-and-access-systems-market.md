<p><p>Vehicle toll collection and access systems can be categorized into several types. Mainline barrier tolls are placed on major highways, charging vehicles as they pass through designated points, often utilizing physical barriers. Entry/exit tolls are used at the beginning and end of specific routes, collecting fees based on distance traveled. Other market types include electronic toll collection systems, which streamline payment processes, and congestion pricing schemes that charge vehicles based on demand during peak times, promoting efficient road usage.</p></p>
